About the project

A house that has
always belonged here.

Hadoar 8 returns one of the city's first addresses to its old function: a place of arrival. Twenty-six private residences inside the building that, a century ago, served as Tel Aviv's first telegraph and post office.

The exterior is by Yaniv Pardo. Every interior — every fixture, every finish, every brass fitting — is by Yaron Eldad. A complete interior architecture, resolved to the last detail.

Architecture · Yaniv Pardo

A dialogue between
old and new.

The complex sits at the heart of the developing Hadoar quarter — an intimate, quiet pocket between the coastline and the renewing Jerusalem Boulevard, framed by restored Ottoman and British-Mandate buildings.

A preserved commercial façade holds the historic street; behind it, three sculpted volumes open through twin patios toward the inner courtyard, the sea, old Jaffa, and the Tel Aviv skyline.
